# Personal-Loan-Acceptance-Prediction
Professional & Management Roles

Management professionals and technicians show above-average subscription rates

Likely have stable income and financial planning awareness

Higher Education Levels

Customers with tertiary education are 2-3x more likely to subscribe

Better understanding of financial products

Middle-Age Group (35-60 years)

Peak earning years with financial stability

More likely to have savings and investment goals

Customers with Higher Balances

Balance > $2,000 shows significantly higher subscription rates

Indicates financial capacity and savings habit

Married Individuals

Slightly higher propensity, possibly due to family financial planning

Low-Probability Segments to Avoid:
Blue-collar workers and unemployed individuals

Customers with negative balances or very low balances

Younger customers (<25 years) with limited financial history

Marketing Strategy Recommendations:
Targeted Campaigns: Focus on management professionals aged 35-60 with tertiary education

Balance Screening: Prioritize customers with balances above $1,000

Educational Content: Develop financial literacy materials for educated professionals

Channel Optimization: Use direct contact methods for high-value segments

Risk Management: Avoid extensive resources on blue-collar and unemployed segments

The Decision Tree model performed slightly better for this imbalanced dataset, achieving approximately 89% accuracy with good recall for the positive class. The most important features were duration, balance, age, and campaign parameters.

This analysis provides a data-driven foundation for optimizing marketing resources and improving conversion rates for personal loan offers.
